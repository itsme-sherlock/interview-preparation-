## Challenges & Problem Solving

### What Is The Most Challenging Project You Have Worked On?

> One of the most challenging projects I worked on was a sublease invoice enhancement. The requirement involved analyzing large volumes of data, understanding complex business calculations, optimizing database performance, and implementing changes without breaking existing functionality. The key challenge was balancing feature development with performance—I had to write efficient PL/SQL code that handled millions of records while meeting strict performance requirements. I worked closely with business analysts to understand the requirements deeply and with DBAs to optimize the solution.

### Tell Me About The Biggest Challenge You Faced

> One of the biggest challenges I faced was resolving a critical production issue related to the Import Export Depot Allotment screen. We had introduced additional search options using booking numbers, which inadvertently caused a massive performance degradation. Users couldn't access the screen because queries were taking minutes to complete. I analyzed the execution plans, identified missing indexes and suboptimal joins, and implemented fixes that brought query response time from 3+ minutes back to under 5 seconds. This taught me the importance of performance testing before deployments and understanding database internals.

### Tell Me About A P1 Incident

> A P1 incident I handled involved duplicate results being displayed in a business-critical screen after a production deployment. Since users were actively using the screen, it required immediate attention. I quickly reviewed the recent code changes, identified a logic error in a new feature, and deployed a hotfix within 30 minutes. The incident taught me the importance of thorough code reviews, comprehensive testing, and having a rapid response process for critical issues.

## Technical Experience

### Tell Me About A Project You Built From Scratch

> I developed an Oracle Forms module from scratch based on a business requirement. The solution involved around six to seven canvases, interactions with multiple database tables, and backend PL/SQL logic. I handled the complete lifecycle—understanding requirements, designing the data model, building the UI/UX, writing PL/SQL, testing, and deploying to production. This project taught me how to think end-to-end about solutions and take full ownership from conception to deployment.

### Tell Me About Query Optimization Work

> I have worked on several query optimization activities where users experienced slow application performance. My approach is to analyze execution plans, review joins and filtering conditions, identify missing indexes, and refactor complex queries. In one case, I optimized a report query from 2 minutes to 3 seconds by adding strategic indexes and rewriting subqueries. I regularly use tools like EXPLAIN PLAN to understand query execution and work with DBAs to implement indexing strategies. Query optimization has become one of my key areas of expertise.

### Tell Me About Your AI Experience

> I have actively explored AI and automation initiatives. One project involved creating a Git delivery automation agent that helped reduce deployment-related mistakes by validating naming conventions, checking commit messages, and automating repetitive tasks. This experience exposed me to automation, scripting, and thinking about how technology can solve operational problems. I'm eager to explore more AI and automation opportunities in my next role.

### Have You Mentored Others?

> Yes. Junior developers and peers frequently approach me for assistance with Oracle Forms, PL/SQL development, debugging, and understanding business processes. I enjoy sharing knowledge and helping others grow. I believe good mentoring involves not just giving answers, but teaching people how to approach problems systematically. I've helped junior developers deliver their first features, understand complex requirements, and improve their technical skills.
